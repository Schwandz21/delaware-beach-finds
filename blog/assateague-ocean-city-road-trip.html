Assateague and Ocean City in One Road Trip: Wild Coast First, Boardwalk Second

Combine Assateague Island and Ocean City, Maryland, in a balanced road trip from Delaware with wildlife, beach time and boardwalk energy.

By Michael SchwanderPublished July 15, 20269 min read

Assateague and Ocean City work because they are opposites. One is protected barrier-island landscape with wild horses and a strong leave-no-trace ethic. The other is a dense resort town built around beach, food, amusements and boardwalk energy. Trying to make them feel the same misses the entire point.

The correct order

Start with Assateague when energy, patience and daylight are strongest. Finish in Ocean City when your group is ready for food, noise and easier entertainment. Reversing the order can make the protected landscape feel like an afterthought.

Assateague expectations

Assateague Island National Seashore is wildlife habitat, not a petting zoo or staged horse encounter. The National Park Service instructs visitors to keep a safe distance from wild horses and other wildlife, secure food and respect closures and regulations.

  • Check current park alerts and conditions.
  • Expect sun, wind, insects and limited shade.
  • Do not approach or feed horses.
  • Pack out what you bring.

Ocean City finish

After the quieter landscape, Ocean City supplies the contrast: boardwalk, food, amusements and a larger resort-town scale. Pick one or two anchors instead of trying to consume the entire town in a few hours.

One bag for two different coasts

Pack the nature portion first—water, sun protection, bug protection and closed storage—then add a clean layer and small boardwalk budget.

Safety and respect

Weather, surf, heat and wildlife conditions should control the plan. Use official NPS and Ocean City sources for current information. This is especially important because a calm-looking barrier island can still present heat, surf and storm hazards.

What to bring

  • Plenty of water.
  • Sun and insect protection.
  • Food secured from wildlife.
  • Binoculars or a zoom lens instead of approaching animals.
  • Change of clothes for the Ocean City portion.
  • Downloaded maps and current alerts.
