Cape May from Delaware: Turn the Ferry into the Best Part of the Day Trip

Plan a Cape May day trip from Lewes using the Cape May–Lewes Ferry, with timing, walk-on versus car decisions and a realistic itinerary.

By Michael SchwanderPublished July 15, 20269 min read

Cape May is not merely “near” Delaware—it is connected by an 85-minute crossing that can make the transportation part of the trip. The right decision is whether you want the ferry to deliver you and your car into a full Cape May day, or whether the crossing itself is the main experience.

Why the ferry works

The Cape May–Lewes Ferry connects Lewes, Delaware, and Cape May, New Jersey, across Delaware Bay. The operator describes the crossing as approximately 85 minutes. That creates a rare day-trip advantage: water views, lighthouses and the possibility of wildlife before you reach the destination.

Walk-on or bring the vehicle?

Walk on when:

  • The ferry experience is the main event.
  • You have arranged transportation or a seasonal shuttle on the Cape May side.
  • You want a lighter, more relaxed day.

Bring the vehicle when:

  • You want maximum control over Cape May locations.
  • Your group has mobility, kid or gear considerations.
  • You plan to explore beyond a compact downtown route.

Schedules, shuttle availability, reservations, fares and arrival requirements change. Check the ferry directly before building the day.

The ferry-day carry

Use a compact tote, wind layer, sun protection, battery pack and one Delaware-to-Jersey souvenir budget.

A realistic sample itinerary

  1. Arrive at the Lewes terminal with the lead time required by the operator.
  2. Use the crossing as downtime: deck views, photos and a deliberate break from driving.
  3. Choose one Cape May anchor—historic streets, beach, lighthouse area or a food-and-shopping route.
  4. Leave buffer time. Missing a return sailing destroys the relaxed part of the plan.
  5. Finish the return crossing at sunset when scheduling and conditions align.

What to verify

  • Current sailing schedule and reservation status.
  • Vehicle or walk-on rules.
  • Seasonal transportation on the New Jersey side.
  • Weather and service alerts.
  • Parking and destination hours.
