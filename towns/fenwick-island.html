The Last Mile Before Maryland

Fenwick Island

A quiet lighthouse town at Delaware's southern edge, close enough to Ocean City to borrow its energy without keeping it.

Delaware

Fenwick sits right on the Delaware-Maryland line, and it uses that position well: a calm, residential beach town during the day, with Ocean City's boardwalk and bigger-city energy fifteen minutes south whenever you want it.

The town itself is unhurried — the historic Fenwick Island Lighthouse still stands watch a few blocks from the beach, and the sand rarely feels crowded even in the middle of August. It's a base camp more than a destination, and that's precisely its appeal.

Golden hour at the lighthouse, looking back north up the coastline toward Bethany — fewer people photograph this stretch than almost anywhere else on the Delaware shore.

Know before you go

  • The state park beach at the Maryland line has some of the least crowded sand in the area.
  • Ocean City's boardwalk is a short drive south when you want more going on.
  • Fenwick's public beach access points are underused compared to towns just north — easy parking most mornings.
